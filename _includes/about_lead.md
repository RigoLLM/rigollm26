These challenges stem from the inherent stochasticity of learning algorithms and the
pervasive nondeterminism in modern LLM systems — amplified by rapidly evolving hardware,
distributed execution, and complex software toolchains. As a result, today's LLM systems
often rely on ad hoc testing, heuristics, and trust in opaque components.

RigoLLM brings together researchers and practitioners from **systems, machine learning,
verification, and programming languages** to examine foundations, tools, and methodologies
for rigor in LLM systems. It is a forum to articulate open problems, share early results,
and shape a research agenda toward LLM systems that can be systematically checked, audited,
and trusted.
